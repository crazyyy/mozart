Our Musicians
EDQ

is a young and vibrant woodwind quintet made up of musicians who are passionate about establishing the wind chamber music scene in Singapore, includes musicians from SSO

Accordion Singapore Ensemble,

professional accordionists from China, Russia and France play in various stages in Singapore and overseas familiar music of different styles. Benefiting from both Caribbean and French musical culture, Linius played various styles inside the Martiniquan orchestra of Roger Chiapello and Charles Kondoky's orchestra. Jiaxi is one of the young accordionists from the city of Xi’an.

He began his accordion journey at the age of five when he enrolled in the accordions’ group of the Children's Palace of his city. Sasha is one of the known musicians in his home city of Saint - Petersburg. In 2012 he was appointed as a resident accordionist and the accordionist ensemble conductor in Saint - Petersburg Rimsky-Korsakov music school. He is the co-founder and leader of the "Accordions Singapore" accordionists interest community and "Accordion Singapore Ensemble”.

@Libitum Quartet

is a professional Russian string quartet with an aim to make "classical" music of the past accessible once again to the modern listener.

Fontane Liang, harpist

Born in Singapore, is the pioneer young harpist of her generation in her country.

She won various scholarships, including the Singapore Arts Council Overseas Merit Bursary, to study at the Guildhall School of Music & Drama, where Fontane also received her Masters in Music Therapy.

She splits her time between London and her native Singapore.

Anna Ivanenko, vocal

was born in Russia.
Before coming to Singapore she was working as a Lead Opera Vocalist in Magnitogorsk State Opera and Ballet Theatre in Russia from 2003 till 2010.

Anna performed roles in various operas, operettas, musicals and music performances for children.

Roman Tarasov,guitar

Has an extensive teaching and performing schedule and is specialized in both classical/acoustic and electric guitar.

He is a permanent member of concert groups such as Firebrands and Shades in Grey, having performed not only in Singapore but also in India, Vietnam, Taiwan, Japan, Australia, UAE and the USA.

Guest Artists
The unique duet of violinists "Kazanskie Brothers"

Vigorous emotional direction of performances, communication with the audience, drive, vivid emotions, good mood, and, obviously, unlimited devotion to music contribute a lot to distinction of the style of this duet. Having begun their career more than 12 years ago in the Central School of Music by Moscow State Conservatory named after P. Tchaikovsky, these guys managed to preserve their attachment to the cause of violin.

As they were honing their skills at Conservatory of Venice, living hand in hand with each other, Kazanskie brothers decided to perform together in the duet. "Due to our firm base we were able to perform at classical concerts and festivals, however, we felt that our potential contains something more than performance at a common concert hall. Therefore, we started to seek "our own music" and perform it elsewhere we could: in the streets, parks, places for those who are as young, vigorous, energetic, free from stereotypes as we are!" - these guys tell us.
